I design and build for the web, living the dream with the design team at GitHub.

I've been passionate about creating and designing content for the web since the day my parents came home with a computer that could connect to the internet. During high school, my friends and I started a small web design company and built websites for local businesses. After attending college at Drexel University in Philadelphia, I cut my teeth at a small startup in New York City before persuing music. After a few years trying to make it in a band (and becoming very broke), I returned to tech and began working for digital agencies in Philadelphia. During my time in client services, I worked with clients like The American Red Cross, DirecTV, Google, Weil Gotshal & Manges, Trek Bicycles, W.L. Gore & Associates, Philly.com, and The US Open. Almost a decade later, I joined the friendly folks at GitHub to help shape the future of software development.

Staying passionate, sharp, and collaborative

The drive to make, learn, and share things keeps me interested in my work. Here are some examples of projects that I've worked on outside of my job.

Aware

Aware is a menubar app for OSX that tracks and displays how long you've been actively using your computer.

Tab Lab

Tab Lab is a web app created to publish and collaborate on guitar tablature and sheet music.

Quirks & Compulsions

The show about overanalyzing the overlooked. With my co-hosts, Anthony Colangelo and Aura Seltzer, we discuss their obsessions with life's minutiae.
