I’m a designer. I build products and brands.

I’ve made my career working with startups, Fortune 500 companies, and all types of organizations in between.

Currently, I’m designing at GitHub, helping to shape the future of collaboration in the software development space. Previously, I worked on the design teams at Happy Cog and Empathy Lab. I also moonlight as a freelancer when I find projects that are a good fit.

My journey as a web designer began in the mid-1990’s. The web was approaching its tenth birthday. I was barely a teenager. My family had just bought a computer that could connect to the internet at a blazing 28.8kbps. It was an exciting time. One day after school I started experimenting with a free WYSWIG editor to publish a list of Magic the Gathering cards that I wanted. Within a few months, I was ducking in and out of IRC channels seeking more information about publishing on the web. By early high school, I traded in the Magic cards for an endeavor worth infinitely more adolescent social capital — playing the drums in a band. Armed with original content, I got hooked on designing, building, and sharing it with the world.

My musical background

I grew up playing music. When I was seven years old I started taking piano lessons, which soon gave way to bass and drum lessons. From the time I was a teenager I played drums and sang in bands and dreamt of someday making a career out of it. In 2003 until 2012, I toured throughout the United States and Canada between freelance jobs and on vacation days. I was lucky enough to be in two bands that got record deals on indie labels.
