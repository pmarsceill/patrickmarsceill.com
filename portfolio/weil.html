Weil, Gotshal & Manges is one of the largest lawfirms in the world. With 20 offices spanning 3 continents and 9 countries, their content needs were unlike any I’ve ever designed for.

Weil’s clients include businesses across many industries and countries, partnering with people with an array of job titles. Serving such a diverse group meant spotlighting utility and wayfinding as a key requirement. Behind the design, our team architected a set of content relationships between people, business sectors, practice groups, and locations to provide the backbone for this rich responsive experience.

With a history deeply rooted in diversity, Weil's new site had to put its people at the forefront.

Our process began with a content strategy phase, giving way to many sketches of the system showing how different content relationships could be formed. I worked on building a set of HTML wireframes that evolved into production code as we moved further along through the design process.
